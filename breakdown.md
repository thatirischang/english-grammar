<h3>S-001 · Simple Present (一般现在时)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Money</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">tells</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">a story</span>; <span class="s" data-tag="S">the dollar</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">tells</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">one of the longest</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = Money / the dollar &nbsp; V = tells &nbsp; O = a story / one of the longest</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Simple Present, used here for an aphoristic / general truth. The semicolon ties two parallel clauses.<br><b>语法:</b> 一般现在时,用于陈述普遍真理或格言式表述。分号连接两个平行的主谓宾结构。</p>
<p class="zh">〔金钱〕〔讲述〕〔一个故事〕；〔美元〕〔讲述〕〔其中最漫长的一个〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 金钱 / 美元 &nbsp; 谓 = 讲述 &nbsp; 宾 = 一个故事 / 其中最漫长的一个</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-002">
<h3>S-002 · Historic / Narrative Present (历史现在时)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">The year is 1792</span>, and <span class="s" data-tag="S">a young republic</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">stamps out</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">its first silver dollar</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">at the Philadelphia mint</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A (time) = The year is 1792 &nbsp; S = a young republic &nbsp; V = stamps out &nbsp; O = its first silver dollar &nbsp; A (place) = at the Philadelphia mint</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Historic Present — past events told in the present tense for vividness. Common in journalism, narrative essays, history-writing.<br><b>语法:</b> 历史现在时(亦称叙事现在时)。用一般现在时讲述过去事件,使叙事更具临场感,常见于新闻、叙事散文、历史写作。</p>
<p class="zh">〔时为 1792 年〕,〔年轻的共和国〕〔在费城造币厂〕〔铸下〕〔它的第一枚银元〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状(时间) = 时为 1792 年 &nbsp; 主 = 年轻的共和国 &nbsp; 谓 = 铸下 &nbsp; 宾 = 它的第一枚银元 &nbsp; 状(地点) = 在费城造币厂</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-003">
<h3>S-003 · Simple Past (一般过去时)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">On April 2 of that year</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">Alexander Hamilton</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">signed</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">the Coinage Act</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">into law</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A = On April 2 of that year &nbsp; S = Alexander Hamilton &nbsp; V = signed &nbsp; O = the Coinage Act &nbsp; A (result) = into law</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Simple Past, the basic narrative tense for a single completed action with a definite past time.<br><b>语法:</b> 一般过去时,叙事的基本时态,描述某个明确过去时间点上完成的动作。</p>
<p class="zh">〔那年 4 月 2 日〕,〔亚历山大 · 汉密尔顿〕〔将《铸币法案》签署〕〔为法律〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状 = 那年 4 月 2 日 &nbsp; 主 = 亚历山大 · 汉密尔顿 &nbsp; 谓 = 签署 &nbsp; 宾 = 《铸币法案》 &nbsp; 补 = 为法律</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-004">
<h3>S-004 · Past Continuous (过去进行时)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">While <span class="s" data-tag="S">Hamilton</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">was building</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">a national bank</span></span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">Thomas Jefferson</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">was drafting</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">the very arguments meant to one day destroy it</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A (time-clause) = While Hamilton was building a national bank &nbsp; S = Thomas Jefferson &nbsp; V = was drafting &nbsp; O = the very arguments [meant to one day destroy it]</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Past Continuous, used to mark two simultaneous, ongoing past actions — typically introduced by <em>while</em>. The reduced relative <em>meant to one day destroy it</em> uses a past participle as adjective.<br><b>语法:</b> 过去进行时,用于描述两个同时发生、持续进行中的过去动作,常以 while 引导。<em>meant to ... destroy it</em> 是省略关系代词的过去分词作定语。</p>
<p class="zh">〔当〔汉密尔顿〕〔在建立〕〔国家银行〕之时〕,〔托马斯 · 杰斐逊〕〔正在起草〕〔日后注定将摧毁它的那些主张〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状(从句) = 当汉密尔顿在建立国家银行之时 &nbsp; 主 = 托马斯 · 杰斐逊 &nbsp; 谓 = 正在起草 &nbsp; 宾 = 日后注定将摧毁它的那些主张</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-005">
<h3>S-005 · Past Perfect (过去完成时)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">By 1811</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">Spanish reales</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">had circulated</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">through the thirteen colonies</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">for nearly a century</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A (time-anchor) = By 1811 &nbsp; S = Spanish reales &nbsp; V = had circulated &nbsp; A (place) = through the thirteen colonies &nbsp; A (duration) = for nearly a century</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Past Perfect — an action that occurred or extended <em>before</em> a past reference point (1811). <em>By + time</em> is the classic anchor.<br><b>语法:</b> 过去完成时,表示在过去某个参照时间点(1811)之前已发生或一直延续的动作。"By + 时间"是典型的时间锚点。</p>
<p class="zh">〔到 1811 年时〕,〔西班牙银币〕〔在十三个殖民地〕〔已流通了近一个世纪〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状(时间锚点) = 到 1811 年时 &nbsp; 主 = 西班牙银币 &nbsp; 谓 = 已流通(过完时) &nbsp; 状 = 在十三个殖民地 &nbsp; 状 = 近一个世纪</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-006">
<h3>S-006 · Past Perfect Continuous (过去完成进行时)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">For two decades</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">the Jeffersonians</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">had been warning</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">their countrymen</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">against centralized finance</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A (duration) = For two decades &nbsp; S = the Jeffersonians &nbsp; V = had been warning &nbsp; O = their countrymen &nbsp; A = against centralized finance</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Past Perfect Continuous emphasises the <em>duration</em> of an activity continuing up to a past point. Combines past + perfect + progressive aspect.<br><b>语法:</b> 过去完成进行时,强调动作在过去某时点之前已"持续进行"了一段时间。融合了"过去 + 完成 + 进行"三种体。</p>
<p class="zh">〔二十年来〕,〔杰斐逊派人士〕〔一直在向〕〔他们的同胞〕〔警告中央集权式金融的危险〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状(时长) = 二十年来 &nbsp; 主 = 杰斐逊派人士 &nbsp; 谓 = 一直在警告(完进时) &nbsp; 宾 = 他们的同胞 &nbsp; 状 = 关于中央集权金融</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-007">
<h3>S-007 · Used to (过去常做)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">American farmers</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">used to carry</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">tobacco notes, Spanish coin, and even gold dust</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">as everyday money</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = American farmers &nbsp; V = used to carry &nbsp; O = tobacco notes, Spanish coin, and even gold dust &nbsp; A = as everyday money</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>used to</em> + bare infinitive — past habit or state, no longer true. Distinct from <em>be used to + V-ing</em> (= accustomed to).<br><b>语法:</b> "used to + 动词原形",表示过去经常做但现在已不再做的习惯。注意与 "be used to + V-ing"(习惯于)区分。</p>
<p class="zh">〔美国农人〕〔过去常〕〔把烟草票、西班牙硬币,甚至金粉〕〔当作日常货币〕〔携带〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 美国农人 &nbsp; 谓 = 过去常携带 &nbsp; 宾 = 烟草票、西班牙硬币、金粉 &nbsp; 状 = 当作日常货币</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-008">
<h3>S-008 · Would (Habitual Past · 表示过去习惯的 would)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">On Saturday mornings</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">a country shopkeeper</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">would shave</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">a thin sliver</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">off any silver dollar he did not trust</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A (time) = On Saturday mornings &nbsp; S = a country shopkeeper &nbsp; V = would shave &nbsp; O = a thin sliver &nbsp; A = off any silver dollar he did not trust (zero-relative)</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>would</em> for past habitual / repeated action — interchangeable with <em>used to</em> when the action is repeated, but unlike <em>used to</em>, it cannot describe past states.<br><b>语法:</b> "would" 表示过去反复发生的习惯性动作。与 "used to" 在表"过去习惯动作"时可互换,但 "would" 不能表过去状态。"any silver dollar he did not trust" 是省略关系代词的接触从句。</p>
<p class="zh">〔每逢周六上午〕,〔乡镇店主〕〔总会从他不信任的任何一枚银元上〕〔削下〕〔一片薄薄的银屑〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状(时间) = 每逢周六上午 &nbsp; 主 = 乡镇店主 &nbsp; 谓 = 总会削下(过去习惯) &nbsp; 宾 = 一片薄薄的银屑 &nbsp; 状 = 从他不信任的任何一枚银元上</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-009">
<h3>S-009 · Restrictive Relative + Preposition Fronted (限定性定语从句 · 介词前置)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">The dollar <span class="m" data-tag="M">that Hamilton designed</span></span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">weighed</span> <span class="c" data-tag="C">exactly 416 grains</span>, <span class="m" data-tag="M">of which 371.25 grains were pure silver</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = The dollar [that Hamilton designed] &nbsp; V = weighed &nbsp; C = exactly 416 grains &nbsp; non-restrict relative with prep-fronted = of which 371.25 grains were pure silver</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Two relative-clause moves in one sentence — (a) restrictive <em>that Hamilton designed</em> defining <em>the dollar</em>; (b) non-restrictive <em>of which …</em> with the preposition <em>of</em> fronted before the relative pronoun. Formal register.<br><b>语法:</b> 一句内两种定语从句:(a) 限定性 <em>that Hamilton designed</em> 限定 <em>the dollar</em>;(b) 非限定性 <em>of which ...</em> ,介词 <em>of</em> 前置在关系代词之前,典型的正式书面语。</p>
<p class="zh">〔汉密尔顿设计的那枚美元〕〔重〕〔恰好 416 格令〕,〔其中 371.25 格令是纯银〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 汉密尔顿设计的那枚美元(含定从) &nbsp; 谓 = 重 &nbsp; 表语/补 = 恰好 416 格令 &nbsp; 非限定性补充 = 其中 371.25 格令是纯银</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-010">
<h3>S-010 · Non-restrictive Relative + as ... as Equality (非限定性定从 + 同级比较)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Andrew Jackson, <span class="m" data-tag="M">who distrusted bankers as deeply as he distrusted aristocrats</span>,</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">vetoed</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">the Second Bank's charter</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">in 1832</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = Andrew Jackson [who ... aristocrats] &nbsp; V = vetoed &nbsp; O = the Second Bank's charter &nbsp; A = in 1832</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Non-restrictive (comma-marked) relative clause adds extra information. Inside it, <em>as deeply as</em> shows equality of degree — the equal-comparative formula.<br><b>语法:</b> 用逗号隔开的非限定性定语从句,补充说明。从句内 "as deeply as ..." 是同级比较,表程度相等。</p>
<p class="zh">〔安德鲁 · 杰克逊,〔他对银行家的不信任与他对贵族的不信任同样深切〕,〕〔在 1832 年〕〔否决了〕〔第二银行的特许状〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 安德鲁 · 杰克逊(含非限定性定从) &nbsp; 谓 = 否决了 &nbsp; 宾 = 第二银行的特许状 &nbsp; 状 = 在 1832 年</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-011">
<h3>S-011 · Inverted Type-3 Conditional + Modal Perfect (倒装第三条件句 + 情态完成式)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">Had Jackson trusted central banking</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">the Panic of 1837</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">might never have torn</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">the frontier</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">apart</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A (cond) = Had Jackson trusted central banking &nbsp; S = the Panic of 1837 &nbsp; V = might never have torn &nbsp; O = the frontier &nbsp; A = apart</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Type-3 conditional (unreal past), with the <em>if</em>-clause inverted: <em>Had + S + V-en</em> replaces <em>If + S + had + V-en</em>. The result-clause uses modal perfect <em>might have V-en</em>.<br><b>语法:</b> 第三条件句(与过去事实相反),if 从句倒装为 "Had + 主语 + 过去分词"形式;主句用情态完成式 "might have + 过去分词"。</p>
<p class="zh">〔倘若杰克逊信任中央银行制度〕,〔1837 年的恐慌〕〔本不会将〕〔边疆〕〔撕裂得四分五裂〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状(虚拟条件,倒装) = 倘若杰克逊信任中央银行制度 &nbsp; 主 = 1837 年的恐慌 &nbsp; 谓 = 本不会撕裂(虚拟过去) &nbsp; 宾 = 边疆 &nbsp; 状 = 四分五裂</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-012">
<h3>S-012 · Cleft Sentence (分裂句 · It-cleft)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="m" data-tag="M">It was</span> <span class="s" data-tag="S">the Civil War, more than any economic doctrine,</span> <span class="m" data-tag="M">that</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">finally drove</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">Washington</span> <span class="c" data-tag="C">to print paper money on a national scale</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">Cleft frame = It was X that ... &nbsp; X (focused) = the Civil War &nbsp; V = drove &nbsp; O = Washington &nbsp; C (object complement) = to print paper money on a national scale</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Cleft sentence (it-cleft) — splits one sentence into two clauses around a focused element <em>X</em>. Emphasises that it was specifically <em>the Civil War</em> (not anything else) that caused the action.<br><b>语法:</b> 分裂句(强调句型)。基本结构 "It was/is + 强调成分 + that/who + 其余部分",将句子拆开以突出某一成分。这里强调"内战",而非任何经济理论。</p>
<p class="zh">〔正是〕〔南北战争 — 而非任何经济理论 — 〕〔最终迫使〕〔华盛顿〕〔在全国范围内印制纸币〕。</p>
<p class="roles">强调框架 = "正是 ... 才 ..." &nbsp; 强调焦点 = 南北战争 &nbsp; 谓 = 迫使 &nbsp; 宾 = 华盛顿 &nbsp; 宾补 = 在全国范围内印制纸币</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-013">
<h3>S-013 · Past Passive + To-infinitive of Purpose (过去时被动 + 不定式表目的)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">The greenback</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">was issued</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">in 1862</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">to finance a war the Treasury could no longer pay for in coin</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = The greenback &nbsp; V = was issued (past passive) &nbsp; A (time) = in 1862 &nbsp; A (purpose) = to finance a war [the Treasury could no longer pay for in coin]</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>was issued</em> = past passive (be + past participle in past tense). The infinitive <em>to finance</em> expresses purpose. Within the object, a contact relative clause (zero-relative) defines <em>a war</em>.<br><b>语法:</b> "was issued" 是一般过去时被动语态(was/were + 过去分词)。"to finance"是不定式表目的。"a war the Treasury could no longer pay for in coin"中省略了关系代词,是接触从句。</p>
<p class="zh">〔绿背钞〕〔在 1862 年〕〔被发行〕,〔为的是支付一场国库已无法再以硬币维持的战争〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 绿背钞 &nbsp; 谓 = 被发行(被动) &nbsp; 状 = 1862 年 &nbsp; 状(目的) = 为了支付一场战争(含定从)</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-014">
<h3>S-014 · Pseudo-cleft / Wh-noun Clause as Subject (假分裂句 · what 引导的主语从句)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">What restored monetary order after Reconstruction</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">was</span> <span class="c" data-tag="C">the Resumption Act of 1875</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = What restored monetary order after Reconstruction (wh-noun clause) &nbsp; V = was (linking verb) &nbsp; C = the Resumption Act of 1875 (predicative)</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Pseudo-cleft (also called wh-cleft). The whole <em>What ...</em> clause functions as the subject; the linking verb <em>was</em> equates it with the focused noun phrase.<br><b>语法:</b> 假分裂句(又称 wh-分裂句)。"What restored ..." 整体充当主语从句,系动词 was 将其与强调焦点(<em>the Resumption Act of 1875</em>)等价对接。</p>
<p class="zh">〔在重建时期之后恢复货币秩序的〕,〔正是〕〔1875 年的《恢复硬币兑付法案》〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主语从句 = 在重建时期之后恢复货币秩序的 &nbsp; 系动词 = 是 &nbsp; 表语 = 1875 年的《恢复硬币兑付法案》</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-015">
<h3>S-015 · Gerund as Subject (动名词作主语)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Returning to the gold standard</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">seemed</span> <span class="c" data-tag="C">prudent to Eastern bankers and ruinous to Western farmers</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = Returning to the gold standard (gerund phrase) &nbsp; V = seemed (linking) &nbsp; C = prudent to Eastern bankers and ruinous to Western farmers</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> A gerund (V-ing) phrase functions as subject. Compare with infinitive-as-subject (<em>To return ...</em>); both work, but the gerund emphasises the activity itself rather than a hypothetical step.<br><b>语法:</b> 动名词短语 (V-ing) 作主语。与不定式作主语 ("To return ...") 类似,但动名词更强调"该活动本身",不定式更指向"做这个动作"。</p>
<p class="zh">〔回到金本位〕,〔在东部银行家眼中显得审慎,在西部农人眼中显得灾难〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主语(动名词短语) = 回到金本位 &nbsp; 谓 = 显得 &nbsp; 表 = 在东部银行家眼中审慎,在西部农人眼中灾难</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-016">
<h3>S-016 · Gerund after Preposition (介词后接动名词)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">By insisting on free silver</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">William Jennings Bryan</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">rallied</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">a generation of debt-stricken farmers</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">behind him</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A (means) = By insisting on free silver &nbsp; S = William Jennings Bryan &nbsp; V = rallied &nbsp; O = a generation of debt-stricken farmers &nbsp; A = behind him</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> After a preposition (here <em>by</em>), the verb must take <em>-ing</em> (gerund) form. <em>by V-ing</em> = "by means of doing." Note also <em>debt-stricken</em>: past-participle compound adjective.<br><b>语法:</b> 介词后必须接动名词(V-ing 形式)。"by + V-ing" = "通过做某事"。注意 <em>debt-stricken</em> 是"名词+过去分词"复合形容词。</p>
<p class="zh">〔通过坚持自由铸银〕,〔威廉 · 詹宁斯 · 布莱恩〕〔在他身后〕〔团结起〕〔整整一代深陷债务的农人〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状(方式) = 通过坚持自由铸银 &nbsp; 主 = 布莱恩 &nbsp; 谓 = 团结起 &nbsp; 宾 = 整整一代深陷债务的农人 &nbsp; 状 = 在他身后</p>

<h3>S-017 · Noun Clause as Object + Modal Passive (宾语从句 + 情态被动)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">In 1896</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">Bryan</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">declared</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">that mankind must not be crucified upon a cross of gold</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A = In 1896 &nbsp; S = Bryan &nbsp; V = declared &nbsp; O = that-clause [mankind must not be crucified upon a cross of gold]</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> The <em>that-</em>clause serves as direct object of <em>declared</em>. Inside the clause, <em>must not be crucified</em> is a modal-passive construction: <em>modal + be + V-en</em>.<br><b>语法:</b> "that mankind ..." 整体作 declared 的直接宾语(宾语从句)。从句内 "must not be crucified" 是情态被动:"情态动词 + be + 过去分词"。</p>

<h3>S-019 · Locative Inversion (地点倒装)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">Out of the Panic of 1907</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">came</span> <span class="s" data-tag="S">the conviction that the country needed a permanent central bank</span>.</p>

<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Locative inversion — when a place / direction adverbial is fronted, the subject and verb invert. Note the long subject (with appositive that-clause) sits at the end for end-weight balance.<br><b>语法:</b> 地点倒装。地点/方向状语前置时,主谓倒装(动词放主语前)。这里被前置的是 "Out of the Panic of 1907",较长的主语挪到句末,符合"末端重心"原则。</p>
<p class="zh">〔从 1907 年的恐慌之中〕〔诞生了〕〔一个信念 — 这个国家需要一家永久性的中央银行〕。</p>

<h3>S-026 · such + Noun ... that (结果状语从句 · such ... that)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">The collapse of October 1929</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">was</span> <span class="c" data-tag="C">such a shock</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">that an entire generation lost faith in finance</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = The collapse of October 1929 &nbsp; V = was &nbsp; C = such a shock &nbsp; A (result) = that an entire generation lost faith in finance</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>such + (a/an) + adj + noun + that-</em>clause — same result-meaning as <em>so ... that</em>, but used with a noun phrase. <div class="bk" id="s-031">
<h3>S-031 · Adverbial Clause of Purpose (目的状语从句 · in order that)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Banks</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">were closed</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">for four days</span>, <span class="a" data-tag="A">in order that public confidence might begin to recover</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = Banks &nbsp; V = were closed &nbsp; A (duration) = for four days &nbsp; A (purpose-clause) = in order that public confidence might begin to recover</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>in order that / so that</em> introduce a purpose clause, usually with a modal (<em>may, might, can, could</em>). Compare to <em>to + V</em> (purpose infinitive, simpler).<br><b>语法:</b> "in order that / so that" 引导目的状语从句,从句中常含情态动词(may, might, can, could)。比"to + 动词"形式更正式、更强调目的。</p>
<p class="zh">〔银行〕〔被关闭〕〔达四天之久〕,〔为的是让公众信心〕〔有机会〕〔开始恢复〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 银行 &nbsp; 谓 = 被关闭 &nbsp; 状(时长) = 达四天 &nbsp; 状(目的从句) = 为的是让公众信心开始恢复</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-032">
<h3>S-032 · Mixed Conditional (混合条件句)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">If the Great Depression had lasted only briefly</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">the dollar</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">today</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">would still hang</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">from a chain of gold</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A (cond, past unreal) = If the Great Depression had lasted only briefly &nbsp; S = the dollar &nbsp; A = today &nbsp; V = would still hang &nbsp; A = from a chain of gold</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Mixed conditional — past condition (<em>had lasted</em>, Type-3 form) plus present result (<em>would hang</em>, Type-2 form). Used when an unreal past would have produced an unreal present consequence.<br><b>语法:</b> 混合条件句。条件部分用第三条件句(had + 过去分词,与过去事实相反),结果部分用第二条件句(would + 动词原形,与现在事实相反)。表"过去若 X,现在就 Y"。</p>
<p class="zh">〔倘若大萧条只持续过短暂时间〕,〔今天的美元〕〔仍会〕〔挂在〕〔一条黄金的链子上〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状(虚拟过去条件) = 倘若大萧条只持续过短暂时间 &nbsp; 主 = 美元 &nbsp; 状 = 今天 &nbsp; 谓 = 仍会挂着(虚拟现在结果) &nbsp; 状 = 一条黄金的链子上</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-033">
<h3>S-033 · "Should" for Advice / Moral Statement (情态动词 should · 建议/道义)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Roosevelt</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">argued</span>, <span class="a" data-tag="A">again and again</span>, <span class="o" data-tag="O">that no civilized nation should ever again allow gold to dictate the limits of human ambition</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = Roosevelt &nbsp; V = argued &nbsp; A = again and again &nbsp; O = that-clause [no civilized nation should ever again allow ...]</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>should</em> expresses moral obligation, recommendation, or advice — softer than <em>must</em>. Inside a noun-clause object after a verb of saying.<br><b>语法:</b> "should" 表示道义责任、建议或忠告,语气比 must 缓和。这里嵌套于"说话动词后的宾语从句"中。</p>
<p class="zh">〔罗斯福〕〔反复〕〔主张〕〔任何文明的国家都不应再让黄金来界定人类抱负的边界〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 罗斯福 &nbsp; 谓 = 主张 &nbsp; 状 = 反复 &nbsp; 宾(从句) = 任何文明的国家都不应再让黄金界定人类抱负的边界</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-034">
<h3>S-034 · Past Participial Phrase (过去分词短语作定语)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">The Bretton Woods system, <span class="m" data-tag="M">designed by Keynes and Harry Dexter White</span>,</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">fixed</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">the world's currencies</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">around a dollar pegged to gold at thirty-five dollars an ounce</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = The Bretton Woods system [+ past-participle phrase: designed by ...] &nbsp; V = fixed &nbsp; O = the world's currencies &nbsp; A = around a dollar [pegged to gold at $35/oz]</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> A past-participle phrase modifies a noun, expressing passive meaning ("which was designed ..."). It works exactly like a reduced relative clause and is often shorter and more elegant.<br><b>语法:</b> 过去分词短语 (V-en + ...) 作定语,带被动意味("which was designed ...")。等价于省略关系代词的定语从句,但更简洁。</p>
<p class="zh">〔由凯恩斯和哈里 · 德克斯特 · 怀特设计的布雷顿森林体系〕,〔将〕〔世界各国货币〕〔锚定在一种以每盎司 35 美元价格挂钩黄金的美元周围〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 布雷顿森林体系(含过去分词定语) &nbsp; 谓 = 锚定 &nbsp; 宾 = 世界各国货币 &nbsp; 状 = 在某种挂钩黄金的美元周围</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-035">
<h3>S-035 · Anticipatory "it" for Object (形式宾语 it)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Keynes</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">found</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">it</span> <span class="c" data-tag="C">impossible</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">to persuade the Americans of his bancor proposal</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = Keynes &nbsp; V = found &nbsp; O (formal) = it &nbsp; C (object complement) = impossible &nbsp; O (real, infinitive) = to persuade ... bancor proposal</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Anticipatory <em>it</em> as formal object. The pattern is <em>find / think / consider / make / believe + it + adj + (real object: to-V or that-clause)</em>. Used because the real object is too long to sit comfortably right after the verb.<br><b>语法:</b> 形式宾语 "it"。结构 "find / think / consider / make / believe + it + 形容词 + 真宾语(不定式或 that 从句)"。因为真宾语太长,放在 it 之后更顺畅。</p>
<p class="zh">〔凯恩斯〕〔觉得〕〔说服美国人接受他的"班柯"方案〕〔是〕〔不可能的〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 凯恩斯 &nbsp; 谓 = 觉得 &nbsp; 形宾 = (省) &nbsp; 宾补 = 不可能的 &nbsp; 真宾(不定式) = 说服美国人接受他的"班柯"方案</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-036">
<h3>S-036 · Whether-Noun Clause as Subject (whether 引导主语从句)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Whether Bretton Woods could survive a quarter-century</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">was</span> <span class="c" data-tag="C">a question its architects scarcely asked</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S (wh-clause) = Whether Bretton Woods could survive a quarter-century &nbsp; V = was &nbsp; C = a question [its architects scarcely asked — zero relative]</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> A <em>whether-</em>clause (yes/no question turned into a noun) functions as subject. <em>Whether</em> can stand at clause-start; <em>if</em> usually cannot when the clause is a subject.<br><b>语法:</b> "whether 从句"(把"是否..."的问句变成名词性结构)作主语。注意:在主语位置只能用 whether,不能用 if。</p>
<p class="zh">〔布雷顿森林体系能否撑过一个四分之一世纪〕,〔是〕〔一个其构建者鲜少自问的问题〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主语(whether 从句) = 布雷顿森林体系能否撑过一个四分之一世纪 &nbsp; 系动 = 是 &nbsp; 表 = 一个其构建者鲜少自问的问题</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-037">
<h3>S-037 · Mandative Subjunctive in Apposition (虚拟语气 · 同位语中)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">The new system</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">rested</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">upon a single condition</span>: <span class="m" data-tag="M">that no one ask for gold</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = The new system &nbsp; V = rested &nbsp; A = upon a single condition &nbsp; Apposition (subjunctive that-clause) = that no one ask for gold</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> A subjunctive that-clause as appositive to an abstract noun (<em>condition, demand, suggestion, requirement, proposal</em>). The verb is bare-infinitive form (here <em>ask</em>, not <em>asks</em>).<br><b>语法:</b> 同位语用法的虚拟语气 that 从句,前接抽象名词(condition / demand / suggestion / requirement / proposal 等)。从句动词用原形(此处 ask 而非 asks)。</p>
<p class="zh">〔这一新体系〕〔仅仰赖于〕〔一个条件〕:〔但凡无人来索取黄金便可〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 这一新体系 &nbsp; 谓 = 仰赖于 &nbsp; 状 = 一个条件 &nbsp; 同位(虚拟从句) = 但凡无人来索取黄金</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-038">
<h3>S-038 · As-If Manner Clause + Were-Subjunctive (as if 从句 + 虚拟过去)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">For two decades</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">the system</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">worked</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">smoothly</span>, <span class="a" data-tag="A">as if gold and the dollar were a single currency</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A = For two decades &nbsp; S = the system &nbsp; V = worked &nbsp; A = smoothly &nbsp; A (manner-clause) = as if gold and the dollar were a single currency</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>as if / as though</em> introduces a manner clause. When the situation is contrary to fact, the verb in the clause shifts back: present → past (<em>were</em>, not <em>was</em>); past → past perfect.<br><b>语法:</b> "as if / as though" 引导方式状语从句,表"似乎,仿佛"。当所述与事实不符时,谓语降一格:现在事实用过去时(用 were 不用 was);过去事实用过去完成时。</p>
<p class="zh">〔二十年间〕,〔这一体系〕〔运转〕〔顺畅〕,〔仿佛黄金与美元〕〔本就是同一种货币〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状 = 二十年间 &nbsp; 主 = 这一体系 &nbsp; 谓 = 运转 &nbsp; 状 = 顺畅 &nbsp; 状(方式 · 虚拟) = 仿佛黄金与美元本就是同一种货币</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-039">
<h3>S-039 · Only-Fronting Inversion (only 前置倒装)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="m" data-tag="M">Only when France began demanding bullion for its dollars</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">did</span> <span class="s" data-tag="S">the cracks</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">become</span> <span class="c" data-tag="C">visible</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">Only-phrase (fronted, restrictive) = Only when France began demanding bullion for its dollars &nbsp; Aux = did &nbsp; S = the cracks &nbsp; V = become &nbsp; C = visible</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> When <em>only + adverbial</em> is fronted, the main clause inverts. Pattern: <em>Only + when/after/if/then ..., aux + S + V</em>. Adds emphasis: "not until X did Y happen."<br><b>语法:</b> "Only + 状语"前置时,主句要倒装。结构:"Only + when/after/if/then ...,助动词 + 主语 + 动词"。强调"直到 X 才 Y"。</p>
<p class="zh">〔只有当法国开始为它的美元索要金条时〕,〔裂缝〕〔才〕〔显现出来〕。</p>
<p class="roles">only 状语(前置) = 只有当法国开始为美元索要金条时 &nbsp; 主 = 裂缝 &nbsp; 谓 = 才显现出来</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-040">
<h3>S-040 · Past Perfect Passive (过去完成时被动)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">By the late 1960s</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">more than half of America's postwar gold</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">had been spent</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">abroad</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A (anchor) = By the late 1960s &nbsp; S = more than half of America's postwar gold &nbsp; V = had been spent (past-perfect passive) &nbsp; A = abroad</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Past Perfect Passive: <em>had + been + past participle</em>. Combines past perfect (action prior to a past time) with passive voice.<br><b>语法:</b> 过去完成时被动:"had + been + 过去分词"。融合过去完成时(更早过去)与被动语态。</p>
<p class="zh">〔到 1960 年代后期〕,〔美国战后黄金的逾一半〕〔已〕〔被〕〔花在〕〔海外〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状(锚) = 到 1960 年代后期 &nbsp; 主 = 美国战后黄金的逾一半 &nbsp; 谓 = 已被花费(过完被) &nbsp; 状 = 海外</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-041">
<h3>S-041 · Wh-Noun Clause as Object (what 引导宾语从句)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">On August 15, 1971</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">Richard Nixon</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">announced</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">what would later be called the Nixon Shock</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A = On August 15, 1971 &nbsp; S = Richard Nixon &nbsp; V = announced &nbsp; O (wh-clause) = what would later be called the Nixon Shock</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> A <em>wh-</em>clause as direct object. <em>what</em> here = "the thing that"; the clause functions as a noun. Inside it, <em>would later be called</em> is future-in-the-past passive.<br><b>语法:</b> wh-从句作宾语。这里 "what" 相当于"the thing that ..."。从句内 "would later be called" 是"过去将来时被动"。</p>
<p class="zh">〔1971 年 8 月 15 日〕,〔理查德 · 尼克松〕〔宣布了〕〔后来被称为"尼克松冲击"的那件事〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状 = 1971 年 8 月 15 日 &nbsp; 主 = 理查德 · 尼克松 &nbsp; 谓 = 宣布了 &nbsp; 宾(从句) = 后来被称为"尼克松冲击"的那件事</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-042">
<h3>S-042 · Present Perfect (现在完成时)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">The dollar</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">has floated</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">freely</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">ever since</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = The dollar &nbsp; V = has floated (present perfect) &nbsp; A = freely &nbsp; A = ever since</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Present Perfect: <em>have/has + past participle</em>. Connects a past beginning to the present. <em>ever since</em> emphasises continuity from a past anchor (here, 1971).<br><b>语法:</b> 现在完成时:"have/has + 过去分词"。连接过去起点与现在。"ever since" 强调"自从某个过去锚点起,一直到现在"。</p>
<p class="zh">〔从那时起〕,〔美元〕〔便始终〕〔自由〕〔浮动〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 美元 &nbsp; 谓 = 便始终浮动(现完时) &nbsp; 状 = 自由 &nbsp; 状 = 从那时起</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-043">
<h3>S-043 · Could (Ability / Possibility · can/could 的能力或可能)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">After 1971</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">a single sentence from a central banker</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">could move</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">trillions of dollars</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">overnight</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A = After 1971 &nbsp; S = a single sentence from a central banker &nbsp; V = could move (modal) &nbsp; O = trillions of dollars &nbsp; A = overnight</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>could</em> as past-tense or hypothetical equivalent of <em>can</em>: ability or possibility. Distinguish from <em>could have V-en</em> (modal perfect, possibility past).<br><b>语法:</b> "could" 表过去的能力或可能,是 can 的过去时形式。注意区分"could + 动词原形"(过去能力/可能)与"could have + 过去分词"(过去本可以)。</p>
<p class="zh">〔1971 年之后〕,〔一位央行行长的一句话〕〔便能〕〔在一夜之间〕〔搬动〕〔万亿美元〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状 = 1971 年之后 &nbsp; 主 = 一位央行行长的一句话 &nbsp; 谓 = 便能搬动 &nbsp; 宾 = 万亿美元 &nbsp; 状 = 一夜之间</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-044">
<h3>S-044 · Present Participial Phrase (现在分词短语作定语)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Inflation, <span class="m" data-tag="M">slumbering since the 1950s</span>,</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">awoke</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">in the 1970s</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">with sudden ferocity</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = Inflation [+ present-participle phrase: slumbering since the 1950s] &nbsp; V = awoke &nbsp; A = in the 1970s &nbsp; A = with sudden ferocity</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Present-participle phrase (V-ing + complements) as adjective, modifying a noun. Active meaning, ongoing aspect — contrasts with the past-participle phrase (S-034) which carries passive meaning.<br><b>语法:</b> 现在分词短语 (V-ing + 补足成分) 作定语修饰名词,具主动、进行的语义。对照 S-034 的过去分词短语(被动语义)。</p>
<p class="zh">〔自 1950 年代起便已蛰伏的通胀〕,〔在 1970 年代〕〔以骤然的凶猛〕〔醒来〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 通胀(含现在分词定语) &nbsp; 谓 = 醒来 &nbsp; 状 = 在 1970 年代 &nbsp; 状 = 以骤然的凶猛</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-045">
<h3>S-045 · Comparative + Than (比较级)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">Year after year</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">prices</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">rose</span> <span class="c" data-tag="C">faster than wages</span>, and <span class="s" data-tag="S">the average American</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">learned to mistrust</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">his own paycheck</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A = Year after year &nbsp; S₁ = prices &nbsp; V₁ = rose &nbsp; C = faster than wages &nbsp; S₂ = the average American &nbsp; V₂ = learned to mistrust &nbsp; O = his own paycheck</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Comparative degree: <em>adj/adv + -er + than</em> (or <em>more + adj/adv + than</em>). Compares two unequal items.<br><b>语法:</b> 比较级:"形容词/副词 + -er + than" 或 "more + 形容词/副词 + than"。用于比较两个不相等的事物。</p>
<p class="zh">〔年复一年〕,〔物价〕〔上涨〕〔得比工资更快〕,〔普通美国人〕〔学会了不再信任〕〔自己的薪水袋〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状 = 年复一年 &nbsp; 主₁ = 物价 &nbsp; 谓₁ = 上涨 &nbsp; 比较 = 比工资更快 &nbsp; 主₂ = 普通美国人 &nbsp; 谓₂ = 学会不再信任 &nbsp; 宾 = 自己的薪水袋</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-046">
<h3>S-046 · Either ... or (相关并列连词)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="m" data-tag="M">Either</span> <span class="s" data-tag="S">confidence</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">had to return</span>, <span class="m" data-tag="M">or</span> <span class="s" data-tag="S">the dollar itself</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">would dissolve</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">Correl₁ = Either &nbsp; S₁ = confidence &nbsp; V₁ = had to return &nbsp; Correl₂ = or &nbsp; S₂ = the dollar itself &nbsp; V₂ = would dissolve</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>Either ... or</em> presents two alternative possibilities. The verb agrees with the noun closer to it (proximity rule). Compare with <em>neither ... nor</em>.<br><b>语法:</b> "Either ... or" 提供两种可能。当连接主语时,谓语与靠近它的那个主语保持一致(就近原则)。与 "neither ... nor" 同结构。</p>
<p class="zh">〔要么〕〔信心〕〔必须回来〕,〔要么〕〔美元自身〕〔将会崩解〕。</p>
<p class="roles">关联词₁ = 要么 &nbsp; 主₁ = 信心 &nbsp; 谓₁ = 必须回来 &nbsp; 关联词₂ = 要么 &nbsp; 主₂ = 美元自身 &nbsp; 谓₂ = 将会崩解</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-047">
<h3>S-047 · "Might" for Possibility (情态动词 might · 可能性)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">For a moment</span>, <span class="m" data-tag="M">it</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">seemed</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">that the dollar might dissolve along with the country's patience</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A = For a moment &nbsp; "It" (anticipatory) + V = it seemed &nbsp; O (real, that-clause) = that the dollar might dissolve along with the country's patience</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>might</em> expresses possibility (a degree weaker than <em>may</em>). Inside a <em>that-</em>clause that is the real subject of <em>it seemed</em>.<br><b>语法:</b> "might" 表"可能",语气比 may 更弱。这里嵌套于 "it seemed that ..." 的真实主语从句中。</p>

<h3>S-058 · Emphatic "do" (强调助动词 do)</h3>

<h3>S-062 · "Shall" (Formal · 表决意 / 法律语气)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Central banks</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">declared</span>, <span class="a" data-tag="A">with rare unanimity</span>, <span class="o" data-tag="O">that the world shall not face another Lehman</span>.</p>

<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>shall</em> in formal / legal / declarative use — expresses a binding commitment or prohibition ("the world shall not ..."). Modern American English largely replaces it with <em>will</em>, but contracts and treaties still use <em>shall</em>.<br><b>语法:</b> "shall" 用于正式 / 法律 / 宣告语境,表达约束性承诺或禁止。现代美式英语口语已多以 will 代之,但条约、合同、法律文本仍保留 "shall"。</p>
<p class="zh">〔各国央行〕〔以罕见的一致〕〔宣布〕〔世界绝不容许再发生一次雷曼〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 各国央行 &nbsp; 谓 = 宣布 &nbsp; 状 = 以罕见的一致 &nbsp; 宾(从句) = 世界绝不容许再发生一次雷曼</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-063">
<h3>S-063 · Future Continuous (将来进行时)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">Within a decade</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">a new species of money</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">will be challenging</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">the dollar's monopoly on global reserves</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A = Within a decade &nbsp; S = a new species of money &nbsp; V = will be challenging (future progressive) &nbsp; O = the dollar's monopoly on global reserves</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Future Continuous: <em>will + be + V-ing</em>. Describes an action in progress at a future time, or a process unfolding over a future stretch.<br><b>语法:</b> 将来进行时:"will + be + V-ing"。表"将来某时正在进行"或"将来一段时间内持续展开"的动作。</p>
<p class="zh">〔再过十年〕,〔一种新形态的货币〕〔将正在〕〔挑战〕〔美元在全球储备上的垄断〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状 = 再过十年 &nbsp; 主 = 一种新形态的货币 &nbsp; 谓 = 将正在挑战(将进时) &nbsp; 宾 = 美元在全球储备上的垄断</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-064">
<h3>S-064 · Neither ... nor (相关并列连词 · 双否)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Bitcoin, <span class="m" data-tag="M">a currency needing neither a government to issue it nor a central bank to control it</span>,</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">was launched</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">on January 3, 2009</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = Bitcoin [+ appositive present-participle phrase: a currency needing neither ... nor ...] &nbsp; V = was launched &nbsp; A = on January 3, 2009</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>neither ... nor</em> joins two negative alternatives — "not X and not Y." Verb agrees with the closer noun (proximity rule). The whole phrase here is embedded inside an appositive participial.<br><b>语法:</b> "neither ... nor" 并列两个否定项,等于"既不 X 也不 Y"。谓语与靠近的那个主语保持一致。这里整体嵌入一个分词同位短语之中。</p>
<p class="zh">〔比特币 — 一种〕〔既不需要〕〔政府发行〕〔也不需要〕〔央行管控的货币〕,〔于 2009 年 1 月 3 日〕〔被启动〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 比特币(含同位分词短语) &nbsp; 谓 = 被启动 &nbsp; 状 = 于 2009 年 1 月 3 日</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-065">
<h3>S-065 · Future Simple "will" (一般将来时)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">The dollar</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">will outlive</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">the men who fear for its life</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = The dollar &nbsp; V = will outlive (simple future) &nbsp; O = the men [who fear for its life]</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Simple Future: <em>will + V</em>. Used for predictions, promises, or instant decisions. Compare with <em>be going to + V</em> (more for plans / present evidence — see S-066).<br><b>语法:</b> 一般将来时:"will + 动词原形"。用于预测、承诺、当场决定。注意区别 "be going to + V"(更指计划或基于当前迹象的判断,见 S-066)。</p>
<p class="zh">〔美元〕〔将活得〕〔比那些为它担忧的人更长久〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 美元 &nbsp; 谓 = 将活得更长(一般将来) &nbsp; 宾 = 那些为它担忧的人</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-066">
<h3>S-066 · Be going to (将来时 · 计划/迹象)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Some economists</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">insist</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">that, sooner or later, every government is going to issue a digital version of its own currency</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = Some economists &nbsp; V = insist &nbsp; O (that-clause) = ... every government is going to issue a digital version of its own currency</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>be going to + V</em> — a future tense expressing intention/plan or a prediction supported by present evidence. Less spontaneous than <em>will</em>; more "the writing is on the wall."<br><b>语法:</b> "be going to + 动词原形" 表将来,用于已有计划的意图,或基于当下迹象的预判。比 "will" 更显"已成定局"语气。</p>
<p class="zh">〔有些经济学家〕〔坚持认为〕,〔迟早每一个政府都会发行其本国货币的数字版本〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 有些经济学家 &nbsp; 谓 = 坚持认为 &nbsp; 宾(从句) = 迟早每一个政府都会发行其本国货币的数字版本</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-067">
<h3>S-067 · Future Perfect (将来完成时)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">By 2030</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">every major central bank</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">will have launched</span>, <span class="m" data-tag="M">or at least tested</span>, <span class="o" data-tag="O">its own central-bank digital currency</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A = By 2030 &nbsp; S = every major central bank &nbsp; V = will have launched (future perfect, with parallel ellipsis "or at least tested") &nbsp; O = its own central-bank digital currency</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Future Perfect: <em>will + have + past participle</em>. Marks an action that will be complete <em>before</em> a future reference point. Anchored by <em>by + future-time</em>.<br><b>语法:</b> 将来完成时:"will + have + 过去分词"。表示在将来某个参照点之前已完成的动作。以"by + 将来时间"为锚。</p>
<p class="zh">〔到 2030 年〕,〔每一家主要的央行〕〔都将已经〕〔启动 — 或至少试运行 — 〕〔它自己的央行数字货币〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状 = 到 2030 年 &nbsp; 主 = 每一家主要的央行 &nbsp; 谓 = 都将已经启动(将完时) &nbsp; 宾 = 它自己的央行数字货币</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-068">
<h3>S-068 · Future Perfect Continuous (将来完成进行时)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">By the time you finish reading this article</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">the world</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">will have been generating</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">new dollars, new debts, and new questions</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">every second</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A (anchor) = By the time you finish reading this article &nbsp; S = the world &nbsp; V = will have been generating (future perfect progressive) &nbsp; O = new dollars, new debts, and new questions &nbsp; A = every second</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Future Perfect Continuous: <em>will + have + been + V-ing</em>. Marks an activity that will have been ongoing up to a future reference point. The rarest of the twelve English tenses.<br><b>语法:</b> 将来完成进行时:"will + have + been + V-ing"。表示在将来某个参照点之前一直持续进行的动作。十二大时态中最少见的一种。</p>
<p class="zh">〔到你读完这篇文章时〕,〔这个世界〕〔将一直在〕〔每一秒〕〔生成〕〔新的美元、新的债务和新的问题〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状(锚) = 到你读完这篇文章时 &nbsp; 主 = 这个世界 &nbsp; 谓 = 将一直在生成(将完进时) &nbsp; 宾 = 新的美元、新的债务和新的问题 &nbsp; 状 = 每一秒</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-069">
<h3>S-069 · Future Passive (一般将来时被动)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">If the dollar ever does lose its reserve status</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">every commodity, every loan, and every contract on earth</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">will be rewritten</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A (cond) = If the dollar ever does lose its reserve status (note the emphatic <em>does</em> in a conditional) &nbsp; S = every commodity, every loan, and every contract on earth &nbsp; V = will be rewritten (future passive)</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Future Passive: <em>will + be + past participle</em>. The if-clause inside also uses an emphatic <em>do</em> ("if it ever does lose ...") — combining a Type-1 condition with stylistic emphasis.<br><b>语法:</b> 一般将来时被动:"will + be + 过去分词"。条件从句中的 "ever does lose" 用了强调助动词 do,在第一条件句基础上加了语气强化。</p>
<p class="zh">〔倘若美元真有一天丧失其储备地位〕,〔地球上每一种大宗商品、每一笔贷款、每一份契约〕〔都将被〕〔重写〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状(条件) = 倘若美元真有一天丧失其储备地位 &nbsp; 主 = 每一种大宗商品、每一笔贷款、每一份契约 &nbsp; 谓 = 都将被重写(将被时)</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-070">
<h3>S-070 · Reduced Relative (省略关系代词的定语 · 简化关系从句)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Money <span class="m" data-tag="M">issued by an algorithm rather than a parliament</span></span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">is</span> <span class="c" data-tag="C">something the world has never quite known before</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = Money [+ reduced relative: issued by an algorithm rather than a parliament] &nbsp; V = is &nbsp; C = something [the world has never quite known before — zero-relative]</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Reduced relative — a full relative clause ("which is issued ...") shortened by dropping <em>which/who is</em> and leaving the past participle. Saves words while preserving the modifier's force.<br><b>语法:</b> 简化关系从句 — 完整的定语从句 "which is issued ..." 省略 "which is",只留过去分词。比定语从句简洁,意思不变。</p>
<p class="zh">〔由算法而非议会发行的货币〕,〔是〕〔世界从未真正认识过的东西〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 由算法而非议会发行的货币(简化定语) &nbsp; 系 = 是 &nbsp; 表 = 世界从未真正认识过的东西</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-071">
<h3>S-071 · Adverbial of Condition (条件状语 · provided that)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="a" data-tag="A">Provided that trust survives</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">the dollar</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">will continue to underwrite</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">the architecture of global finance</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">A (cond) = Provided that trust survives &nbsp; S = the dollar &nbsp; V = will continue to underwrite &nbsp; O = the architecture of global finance</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Adverbial conditional clauses can use connectors beyond <em>if</em>: <em>provided (that), providing (that), as long as, on condition that, unless, supposing, in case</em>. <em>Provided that</em> is more formal than <em>if</em>.<br><b>语法:</b> 条件状语从句的连词不止 if 一种,还包括 provided (that) / providing (that) / as long as / on condition that / unless / supposing / in case 等。"Provided that" 比 if 更正式。</p>
<p class="zh">〔只要信任仍存〕,〔美元〕〔仍将继续承保〕〔全球金融的整套架构〕。</p>
<p class="roles">状(条件) = 只要信任仍存 &nbsp; 主 = 美元 &nbsp; 谓 = 仍将继续承保 &nbsp; 宾 = 全球金融的整套架构</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-072">
<h3>S-072 · To-infinitive as Subject (不定式作主语)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">To predict the dollar's future</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">is</span>, <span class="a" data-tag="A">in the end</span>, <span class="c" data-tag="C">to predict the future of the United States itself</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S (to-inf phrase) = To predict the dollar's future &nbsp; V = is &nbsp; A = in the end &nbsp; C (to-inf phrase) = to predict the future of the United States itself</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> A bare to-infinitive phrase as subject. Compare with <em>It is X to V</em> (introductory <em>it</em>) — both work; the bare form is more emphatic and slightly more literary.<br><b>语法:</b> 不定式短语直接作主语。与 "It is X to V"(形式主语 it)相比,不定式直接作主语更强调、更书面/文学化。</p>
<p class="zh">〔预测美元的未来〕,〔归根到底,就是〕〔预测美国本身的未来〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主语(不定式) = 预测美元的未来 &nbsp; 系 = 是 &nbsp; 状 = 归根到底 &nbsp; 表 = 预测美国本身的未来</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-073">
<h3>S-073 · To-infinitive as Object (不定式作宾语)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Most of us</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">have learned</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">to trust the dollar</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">without thinking about why</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = Most of us &nbsp; V = have learned (present perfect) &nbsp; O (to-inf) = to trust the dollar &nbsp; A = without thinking about why (gerund after preposition)</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> A to-infinitive as direct object after verbs like <em>learn, want, hope, decide, plan, refuse, agree, manage, intend</em>. Different verbs choose different object forms: some take to-V, some take V-ing, some both.<br><b>语法:</b> 不定式作宾语,常用动词:learn / want / hope / decide / plan / refuse / agree / manage / intend 等。不同动词搭配不同形式的宾语,需逐个记忆。</p>
<p class="zh">〔我们大多数人〕〔已学会〕〔信任美元〕,〔甚至不再追问为何〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 我们大多数人 &nbsp; 谓 = 已学会(现完时) &nbsp; 宾(不定式) = 信任美元 &nbsp; 状 = 甚至不再追问为何</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-074">
<h3>S-074 · Wish + Past Perfect (虚拟语气 · wish 与过去完成)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">Many a saver in 2008</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">might have wished</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">that he had bought gold at the start of the decade</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = Many a saver in 2008 &nbsp; V = might have wished (modal perfect) &nbsp; O (that-clause, past unreal) = that he had bought gold at the start of the decade</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> <em>wish + that-</em>clause: clause verb "shifts back" — present-unreal → past, past-unreal → past perfect. Here, the wisher in 2008 regrets the past, so the embedded clause uses past perfect (<em>had bought</em>).<br><b>语法:</b> "wish + that 从句" 用虚拟语气:与现在事实相反用过去时,与过去事实相反用过去完成时。这里 2008 年的存款者后悔过去未买黄金,故用过去完成时 had bought。</p>
<p class="zh">〔2008 年的不少存款人,〕〔此时此刻〕〔大概都会希望〕〔自己当初在那个十年的开端就买入了黄金〕。</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 2008 年的不少存款人 &nbsp; 谓 = 大概都会希望(情完时) &nbsp; 宾(虚拟从句) = 自己当初在那个十年的开端就买入了黄金</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-075">
<h3>S-075 · Rhetorical Question (修辞反问句)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="v" data-tag="V">Will</span> <span class="s" data-tag="S">the dollar</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">last</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">another century</span>? — <span class="m" data-tag="M">that</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">no honest economist</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">can answer</span> <span class="a" data-tag="A">with certainty</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">Question form = Will the dollar last another century? &nbsp; Answer-clause: object-fronted demonstrative <em>that</em>, S = no honest economist, V = can answer, A = with certainty</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> A rhetorical question is a question asked for effect rather than for an answer. Often answered immediately by the speaker — here by an emphatic statement of impossibility.<br><b>语法:</b> 修辞反问句 — 提问不是为了得到回答,而是为了表达态度或引出后续陈述。常由说话人自答,如本句紧随其后给出"无人能确切答复"。</p>
<p class="zh">〔美元〕〔会再活〕〔一个世纪吗〕?— 〔这个〕,〔没有一位诚实的经济学家〕〔能〕〔确凿地〕〔回答〕。</p>
<p class="roles">疑问 = 美元会再活一个世纪吗 &nbsp; 答句:宾前置 = 这个 &nbsp; 主 = 没有一位诚实的经济学家 &nbsp; 谓 = 能回答 &nbsp; 状 = 确凿地</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-076">
<h3>S-076 · Vocative / Direct Address (呼语 · 直接称呼读者)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="s" data-tag="S">The dollar</span>, <span class="m" data-tag="M">dear reader</span>, <span class="v" data-tag="V">is</span> <span class="c" data-tag="C">more than money</span>: <span class="s" data-tag="S">it</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">is</span> <span class="c" data-tag="C">a record, a promise, and a memory</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">S = The dollar &nbsp; Vocative = dear reader (set off by commas) &nbsp; V = is &nbsp; C = more than money &nbsp; S₂ = it &nbsp; V₂ = is &nbsp; C₂ = a record, a promise, and a memory</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Vocative — a noun or noun phrase naming the addressee. Always set off by commas. Common in essay or speech to engage the reader/listener directly.<br><b>语法:</b> 呼语(vocative)— 用以称呼听者/读者的名词或名词短语,总以逗号隔开。常见于散文/演讲中拉近距离。</p>
<p class="zh">〔亲爱的读者啊〕,〔美元〕〔不只是〕〔金钱〕:〔它〕〔是〕〔一份记录、一份承诺、一段记忆〕。</p>
<p class="roles">呼语 = 亲爱的读者啊 &nbsp; 主₁ = 美元 &nbsp; 系₁ = 不只是 &nbsp; 表₁ = 金钱 &nbsp; 主₂ = 它 &nbsp; 系₂ = 是 &nbsp; 表₂ = 一份记录、一份承诺、一段记忆</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-077">
<h3>S-077 · Exclamation How / What (感叹句)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="m" data-tag="M">How fragile</span>, and yet <span class="m" data-tag="M">how durable</span>, <span class="s" data-tag="S">this single piece of paper</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">turns out to be</span>!</p>
<p class="roles">Exclamatory frame = How + adj &nbsp; (twice in parallel) &nbsp; S = this single piece of paper &nbsp; V = turns out to be</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Exclamations introduced by <em>How + adj/adv</em> (modifying degree) or <em>What + (a/an) + adj + noun</em>. Word order may invert ("How fragile is this paper!") or remain S-V ("How fragile this paper is!"); both are valid.<br><b>语法:</b> 感叹句两种基本构式:"How + 形容词/副词"(强调程度)与 "What + (a/an) + 形容词 + 名词"。后续可主谓正常或倒装,均可成立。</p>
<p class="zh">〔多么脆弱,又多么坚韧啊,〕〔这薄薄的一张纸〕〔到头来居然如此〕!</p>
<p class="roles">感叹结构 = 多么 + 形容词(并列两次) &nbsp; 主 = 这薄薄的一张纸 &nbsp; 谓 = 到头来居然如此</p>
</div>

<div class="bk" id="s-078">
<h3>S-078 · Tag Question (反意疑问句)</h3>
<p class="en">And <span class="s" data-tag="S">money</span> <span class="v" data-tag="V">tells</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">a story</span>, <span class="m" data-tag="M">doesn't it</span>?</p>
<p class="roles">S = money &nbsp; V = tells &nbsp; O = a story &nbsp; Tag = doesn't it (negative tag for affirmative statement)</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Tag question — a short query appended to a statement to invite agreement. Affirmative statement ⇒ negative tag (<em>tells, doesn't it</em>); negative statement ⇒ affirmative tag. Auxiliary and pronoun must match the main clause.<br><b>语法:</b> 反意疑问句 — 在陈述句末加一句简短反问以征求认同。前肯后否(tells, doesn't it),前否后肯。所用助动词与代词须与主句匹配。</p>
<p class="zh">〔金钱〕〔讲述〕〔一个故事〕,〔不是吗〕?</p>
<p class="roles">主 = 金钱 &nbsp; 谓 = 讲述 &nbsp; 宾 = 一个故事 &nbsp; 反问 = 不是吗</p>
</div>

<h3>S-079 · Interjection (感叹词独立成分 · Ah)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="m" data-tag="M">Ah</span> — and <span class="s" data-tag="S">money</span>, <span class="a" data-tag="A">century after century</span>, <span class="v" data-tag="V">keeps telling</span> <span class="o" data-tag="O">the human story</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">Interjection (independent) = Ah &nbsp; S = money &nbsp; A = century after century &nbsp; V = keeps telling &nbsp; O = the human story</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> An interjection (<em>Ah, Oh, Alas, Indeed, Well, Hush</em>) stands grammatically apart from the sentence — set off by a dash or comma. It conveys emotion or attitude rather than propositional content.<br><b>语法:</b> 感叹词(Ah / Oh / Alas / Indeed / Well / Hush 等)在语法上是独立成分,由破折号或逗号隔开。它传达情感或态度,不参与句子语义结构。</p>
<p class="zh">〔啊〕— 〔金钱〕,〔世代复世代〕,〔不断地讲述着〕〔人类的故事〕。</p>
<p class="roles">感叹词(独立) = 啊 &nbsp; 主 = 金钱 &nbsp; 状 = 世代复世代 &nbsp; 谓 = 不断地讲述着 &nbsp; 宾 = 人类的故事</p>

<h3>S-080 · Formal Subjunctive ("Be it noted" · 化石化虚拟)</h3>
<p class="en"><span class="m" data-tag="M">Be it noted</span>, <span class="m" data-tag="M">then</span>: <span class="s" data-tag="S">the dollar's long day</span>, <span class="a" data-tag="A">like this sentence</span>, <span class="v" data-tag="V">has only just begun</span>.</p>
<p class="roles">Formal subjunctive (fossilised) = Be it noted &nbsp; Discourse = then &nbsp; S = the dollar's long day &nbsp; A = like this sentence &nbsp; V = has only just begun (present perfect)</p>
<p class="grammar"><b>Grammar:</b> Fossilised / formulaic subjunctive — a frozen pattern carried over from older English. Examples: <em>Be it noted, Be it known, Suffice it to say, Long live the king, God save the queen, Come what may, So be it</em>.<br><b>语法:</b> 化石化虚拟语气 — 从古英语沿袭下来的固定形式,已不再"活产"。常见样本:Be it noted / Be it known / Suffice it to say / Long live the king / God save the queen / Come what may / So be it。</p>
<p class="zh">〔须知〕,〔由是〕:〔美元的漫长一日,正如此句,〕〔不过才刚刚开始〕。</p>
<p class="roles">化石化虚拟 = 须知 &nbsp; 语篇 = 由是 &nbsp; 主 = 美元的漫长一日 &nbsp; 状 = 正如此句 &nbsp; 谓 = 不过才刚刚开始(现完时)</p>
