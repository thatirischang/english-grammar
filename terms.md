<p>每个句子可被拆解为若干个<strong>成分</strong>。这一页把英语句子里所有的成分都写一遍 — 中文叫什么、英文叫什么、长什么样、在句子里站哪里、可由什么形态充当。每条都附三例,一例链回 <a href="/breakdown/">breakdown</a> 中的具体句子。</p>

<hr>

<div class="term" id="subject">
<h2>主语 · Subject</h2>
<p class="def">谁(或什么)在做这个动作 / 处于这个状态。一个句子的"出发点"。<br>The person, thing, or idea that the sentence is about.</p>

<p><strong>典型位置:</strong> 在英语限定句中通常位于谓语之前(主谓宾基本语序)。倒装时除外(见状语)。</p>

<p><strong>常见形态:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>名词或名词短语 — <em>The dollar fell.</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-001">S-001</a>)</li>
<li>代词 — <em>They held those dollars …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-054">S-054</a>)</li>
<li>动名词短语 — <em>Returning to the gold standard seemed prudent …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-015">S-015</a>)</li>
<li>不定式短语 — <em>To predict the dollar's future is …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-072">S-072</a>)</li>
<li>that-从句 — <em>That gold survives is no surprise.</em></li>
<li>wh-从句 — <em>What restored monetary order … was the Resumption Act.</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-014">S-014</a>)</li>
<li>whether-从句 — <em>Whether Bretton Woods could survive … was a question …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-036">S-036</a>)</li>
<li>形式主语 it — <em>It seemed that the dollar might dissolve …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-047">S-047</a>)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>主谓一致(Agreement):</strong> 谓语动词的人称/数与主语保持一致。复数主语用复数动词;<em>either ... or / neither ... nor</em> 取就近原则。</p>
</div>

<div class="term" id="predicate">
<h2>谓语 · Predicate / Verb</h2>
<p class="def">告诉我们主语"做什么 / 是什么 / 怎么样"。是句子的核心,围绕动词展开。<br>What the sentence asserts about the subject — built around the main verb.</p>

<p><strong>典型位置:</strong> 紧跟主语之后(SVO 语序)。</p>

<p><strong>构成:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>动词单独</strong> — <em>The dollar fell.</em></li>
<li><strong>助动词 + 主动词</strong>(构成各种时体) — <em>has floated</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-042">S-042</a> · 现在完成);<em>was issued</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-013">S-013</a> · 过去被动);<em>will have been generating</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-068">S-068</a> · 将来完成进行)</li>
<li><strong>系动词 + 表语</strong>(连系结构,描述状态) — <em>The system rested upon a single condition.</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-037">S-037</a>)</li>
<li><strong>情态动词 + 动词原形</strong> — <em>could move</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-043">S-043</a>);<em>must be remembered</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-060">S-060</a>);<em>shall not face</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-062">S-062</a>)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>动词的"五形":</strong> 原形 (do) · 第三人称单数 (does) · 过去 (did) · 现在分词 (doing) · 过去分词 (done)。十六种时态全由这五形 + 助动词构造而出。</p>
</div>

<div class="term" id="object">
<h2>宾语 · Object</h2>
<p class="def">动作所及之物。"V 谁" 或 "V 什么"的那个"谁/什么"。<br>The receiver of the verb's action.</p>

<p><strong>三种宾语:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>直接宾语 (Direct Object) </strong> — <em>Hamilton signed the Coinage Act.</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-003">S-003</a>) "什么被签 = the Coinage Act"</li>
<li><strong>间接宾语 (Indirect Object) </strong> — 双宾语动词(give, send, tell, show, offer ... )之后第一个宾语。如 <em>I sent <u>him</u> a letter.</em></li>
<li><strong>介宾 (Object of a preposition) </strong> — 介词后接的名词/代词。<em>by the Bureau of Engraving</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-020">S-020</a>);<em>upon a single condition</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-037">S-037</a>)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>常见形态(直接宾语)</strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li>名词/代词 — <em>signed the Coinage Act</em></li>
<li>动名词 — <em>began demanding bullion</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-039">S-039</a>)</li>
<li>不定式 — <em>have learned to trust the dollar</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-073">S-073</a>)</li>
<li>that-从句 — <em>declared that mankind must not be crucified …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-017">S-017</a>)</li>
<li>wh-从句 — <em>announced what would later be called the Nixon Shock</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-041">S-041</a>)</li>
<li>形式宾语 it + 真宾(不定式 / 从句) — <em>found it impossible to persuade …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-035">S-035</a>)</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="term" id="complement">
<h2>补语 · Complement</h2>
<p class="def">补全主语或宾语的语义信息。没有它,句子不完整。<br>Completes the meaning of the subject or the object.</p>

<p><strong>主语补语 (Subject Complement) · 表语</strong> — 出现在系动词 (be / become / seem / appear / look / feel / sound / taste / smell / get / grow / turn / remain / stay) 之后,描述主语:</p>
<ul>
<li><em>The new system rested upon a single condition.</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-037">S-037</a>)</li>
<li><em>It was such a shock.</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-026">S-026</a>)</li>
<li><em>The dollar in those years was three things at once.</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-055">S-055</a>)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>宾语补语 (Object Complement) </strong> — 紧跟宾语之后,补足宾语的语义:</p>
<ul>
<li><em>The war made the dollar emerge as a credible reserve currency.</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-022">S-022</a>) <span class="hint">"emerge ..."补充 dollar</span></li>
<li><em>Roosevelt had the confiscated metal reminted.</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-029">S-029</a>) <span class="hint">"reminted"补充 metal</span></li>
<li><em>found it impossible to persuade …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-035">S-035</a>) <span class="hint">"impossible"补充形式宾语 it</span></li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="term" id="adverbial">
<h2>状语 · Adverbial</h2>
<p class="def">交代动作 / 状态的"时间、地点、方式、原因、目的、结果、程度、条件、让步、伴随"。<br>Adds information of <em>when, where, how, why, to what extent, under what condition, despite what</em>.</p>

<p><strong>九大类型(按语义) </strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>时间状语</strong> — 单词:<em>today, ever since, soon</em>;短语:<em>in 1862, by the late 1960s</em>;从句:<em>When the First World War broke out …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-021">S-021</a>)</li>
<li><strong>地点状语</strong> — <em>at the Philadelphia mint, into American vaults, abroad, behind him</em></li>
<li><strong>方式状语</strong> — <em>smoothly</em>;从句 <em>as if gold and the dollar were a single currency</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-038">S-038</a>)</li>
<li><strong>原因状语</strong> — <em>not because they loved Washington, but because they had nowhere else to park their savings.</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-054">S-054</a>)</li>
<li><strong>目的状语</strong> — 不定式:<em>to finance a war …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-013">S-013</a>);从句:<em>in order that public confidence might begin to recover</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-031">S-031</a>)</li>
<li><strong>结果状语</strong> — <em>so confidently that few Americans imagined any cliff ahead</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-025">S-025</a>);<em>such a shock that …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-026">S-026</a>)</li>
<li><strong>程度状语</strong> — <em>almost overnight, at last, very nearly</em></li>
<li><strong>条件状语</strong> — <em>If regulators had looked closer …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-057">S-057</a>);<em>Provided that trust survives …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-071">S-071</a>);<em>Without the Federal Reserve's intervention …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-061">S-061</a>)</li>
<li><strong>让步状语</strong> — <em>Although Bryan lost the election …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-018">S-018</a>)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>状语前置与倒装:</strong> 当地点 / 否定 / "only +" 状语前置时,主谓常需倒装(<a href="/breakdown/#s-019">S-019</a> 地点倒装、<a href="/breakdown/#s-027">S-027</a> nor 倒装、<a href="/breakdown/#s-030">S-030</a> never 倒装、<a href="/breakdown/#s-039">S-039</a> only 倒装)。</p>
</div>

<div class="term" id="modifier">
<h2>定语 / 修饰语 · Modifier · Determiner</h2>
<p class="def">修饰名词或名词短语,告诉读者"哪一个、什么样的、谁的"。<br>Restricts or describes a noun, telling the reader <em>which one, what kind of, whose</em>.</p>

<p><strong>常见形态:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>限定词 — <em>the, a/an, this, that, these, those, my, your, his, every, each, some, any, all, no</em></li>
<li>形容词 — <em>young, postwar, peacetime</em></li>
<li>分词 — <em>confiscated metal</em>(过去分词);<em>floating rates</em>(现在分词)</li>
<li>名词作定语 — <em>silver dollar, gold standard</em></li>
<li>所有格 — <em>America's postwar gold</em></li>
<li>介词短语 — <em>the dollar's monopoly on global reserves</em></li>
<li>不定式 — <em>an attempt to weaken the dollar</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-051">S-051</a>)</li>
<li>过去分词短语 — <em>The Bretton Woods system, designed by Keynes …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-034">S-034</a>)</li>
<li>现在分词短语 — <em>Inflation, slumbering since the 1950s …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-044">S-044</a>)</li>
<li>关系从句 — <em>The dollar that Hamilton designed …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-009">S-009</a>);<em>Andrew Jackson, who distrusted bankers …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-010">S-010</a>)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>限定 vs 非限定:</strong> 限定性定语从句不可省、不加逗号(S-009);非限定性加逗号、可以省(S-010)。</p>
</div>

<div class="term" id="appositive">
<h2>同位语 · Appositive</h2>
<p class="def">两个名词(或名词短语)紧挨,后者重新指称或扩展前者。<br>A noun (phrase) immediately renaming or expanding another.</p>

<p><strong>形式:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>用逗号隔开 — <em>Andrew Jackson, the seventh president, …</em></li>
<li>用破折号 — <em>… Lehman Brothers — once a symbol of American capitalism — filed for bankruptcy.</em></li>
<li>用冒号 — <em>The dollar in those years was three things at once: the currency of America, the language of global trade, and the silent partner in every contract.</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-055">S-055</a>)</li>
<li>无标点紧贴 — <em>my brother John, the great novelist Tolstoy</em></li>
</ul>

<p><strong>同位语从句:</strong> that 引导的从句也可作同位语,扩展抽象名词(<em>fact, idea, condition, hope, news, possibility</em>):<em>The new system rested upon a single condition: that no one ask for gold.</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-037">S-037</a>) — 注意:同位语从句解释名词内容,与定语从句修饰名词限定不同。</p>
</div>

<div class="term" id="independent">
<h2>独立成分 · Independent Elements</h2>
<p class="def">在语法上"独立"于句子主干 — 不充当主、谓、宾、补、状,但出现于句中传递语气、衔接、称呼或感叹。<br>Grammatically free-standing pieces — vocatives, interjections, discourse markers — set off by commas or dashes.</p>

<p><strong>四类:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>呼语 (Vocative)</strong> — 称呼听者/读者。<em>The dollar, dear reader, is more than money.</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-076">S-076</a>)</li>
<li><strong>感叹词 (Interjection)</strong> — 直接表达情绪。<em>Ah — and money, century after century, keeps telling …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-079">S-079</a>);Oh, Alas, Hush, Wow ...</li>
<li><strong>语篇衔接词 (Discourse Marker)</strong> — 表逻辑关系 (Indeed / Moreover / Nevertheless / Therefore / Furthermore / Crucially / Thus)。<em>Indeed, Alan Greenspan presided over a period now called the Great Moderation …</em>(<a href="/breakdown/#s-056">S-056</a>)</li>
<li><strong>插入语 (Parenthetical)</strong> — 评论性插入,常带 "I think / of course / on the whole / to be honest"。也包括 "as you know"、"if I may"等。</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="term" id="absolute">
<h2>独立主格结构 · Absolute Construction</h2>
<p class="def">由"名词 + 分词 / 形容词 / 介词短语"组成的次级结构,自带"小主语",修饰整个主句。<br>A noun-plus-participle (or adj / prep-phrase) clauselet with its own subject, attached loosely to the main clause.</p>

<p><strong>常见样态:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>名词 + 现在分词 — <em>Trust restored, the dollar continued to function as the world's reserve.</em></li>
<li>名词 + 过去分词 — <em>The vote tallied, Roosevelt declared the bank holiday over.</em></li>
<li>名词 + 形容词 — <em>His patience finally exhausted, Volcker raised rates again.</em></li>
<li>With + 独立主格 — <em>With inflation back at three percent, Volcker stepped down in 1987.</em></li>
</ul>

<p><strong>用途:</strong> 用一个紧凑结构补充背景、原因、条件或伴随情况,比从句更简练。文学性较强,口语少见。</p>
</div>

<div class="term" id="five-types">
<h2>五种基本句型 · Five Basic Sentence Patterns</h2>
<p class="def">英语全部句子,只是这五种骨架的扩展。<br>Every English sentence, no matter how long, is built on one of these five skeletons.</p>

<p>所有的从句、修饰、状语、同位、独立成分都是对这五个骨架的<strong>装饰</strong>。先抓骨架,再看装饰 — 这是分析任何复杂英语句子的钥匙。</p>
