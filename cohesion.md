<div class="cohesion-intro">
<p><strong>语篇衔接(cohesion)</strong> 是研究"句子和句子之间靠什么粘起来"的语法层面 — 让一段话读起来是连贯的整体,而不是孤立句子的堆叠。</p>
<p>中国学生最大的英语写作短板就在这一层:<strong>语法考 90 分,作文考 60 分</strong>。差距全在衔接。每句话都对,但段落像散沙 — 这是因为缺少 reference / substitution / ellipsis / conjunction / lexical cohesion 这五大粘合手段。</p>
<p>这一页从《一百零八句拆解》中抽出 <strong>26 个真实衔接案例</strong>,按五大类型重新组织。同样的句子,你将从一个全新的视角看见它们如何"粘"成段落。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-section" id="reference">
<h2>① 指代衔接 · Reference</h2>
<p class="section-intro">用代词、物主代词、指示代词、定冠词,指向上文出现过的事物。让读者通过"回头看"识别同一个对象,免去重复。</p>

<h3>1.1 人称代词回指</h3>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-01">
<h4>C-01 · it 回指上一句的机构</h4>
<p class="coh-en">While Hamilton was building a national bank, Thomas Jefferson was drafting the very arguments meant to one day destroy <strong>it</strong>.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-004">S-004</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">汉密尔顿在搭建国家银行之时,杰斐逊已在起草那些日后注定会将<strong>它</strong>摧毁的论据。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> 一句之内已介绍的核心名词(a national bank),后半句直接用 <code>it</code> 替代,不可重复说"the bank"。重复是中国学生最常见的写作毛病。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-02">
<h4>C-02 · his 物主代词,长距离回指</h4>
<p class="coh-en">Although Bryan lost the election, <strong>his</strong> oratory still echoes through American memory.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-018">S-018</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">尽管布莱恩输掉了那场选举,<strong>他的</strong>雄辩之词至今仍在美国的集体记忆里回响。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> his 一字代替了"Bryan's",节省了一次专有名词的重复。母语者在同段内 70% 用代词回指人,只在新段落开头才用全名。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-03">
<h4>C-03 · it 回指 the dollar(强调主语用代词)</h4>
<p class="coh-en">Bitcoin, a currency needing neither a government to issue <strong>it</strong> nor a central bank to control <strong>it</strong>, was launched on January 3, 2009.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-064">S-064</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">比特币 — 一种既不需要政府发行<strong>它</strong>、也不需要央行管控<strong>它</strong>的货币 — 于 2009 年 1 月 3 日正式启动。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> 同一句中两次回指同一对象(currency),用同一个代词 <code>it</code>。不要写成 "needing neither a government to issue the currency nor a central bank to control the currency" — 重复让句子瘫痪。</p>
</div>

<h3>1.2 the + 词汇回指</h3>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-04">
<h4>C-04 · the country 词汇回指(已知国家)</h4>
<p class="coh-en">Nor did <strong>the country</strong> recover quickly: another decade of stagnation lay ahead.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-027">S-027</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">这个国家也并未迅速复苏 — 又一个十年的停滞,正横亘在前。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> 上文反复提到 the United States,这里用 <code>the country</code> 既保持衔接,又避免词汇重复。母语者会在同义词链中游走:the United States / America / the country / the nation / Washington。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-05">
<h4>C-05 · this single piece of paper 隐性回指(整段话题)</h4>
<p class="coh-en">How fragile, and yet how durable, <strong>this single piece of paper</strong> turns out to be!</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-077">S-077</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">这一张轻薄的纸 — 何其脆弱 — 又何其坚韧!</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> "this single piece of paper" 是对整段话题"美元"的一种**贬抑式回指**,表达写作者的姿态。中国学生很少用这种"以小见大"的指代,值得模仿。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-06">
<h4>C-06 · those 远指 + 限定从句</h4>
<p class="coh-en">The dollar will outlive the men who fear for its life.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-065">S-065</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">美元终将比那些为它担忧的人活得更久。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> "the men who..." 是对群体的精准回指。配 its 反向回指主语(美元)。一句两个指代,流畅而不重复 — 这是英语母语者的本能。</p>
</div>

</div>

<div class="coh-section" id="substitution">
<h2>② 替代衔接 · Substitution</h2>
<p class="section-intro">用 do / so / one 等"占位词"替代上文出现过的整段动作或名词,让句子更紧凑。</p>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-07">
<h4>C-07 · do-substitution(动作替代)</h4>
<p class="coh-en">Where every other currency would have faltered under the same pressure, the dollar somehow <strong>did not</strong>.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-093">S-093</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">在同等压力之下,其他每一种货币本应步履踉跄,而美元,不知何故,竟未动摇。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> "did not" = "did not falter"(falter 被省略,do-not 替代)。中国学生倾向于补全为 "the dollar somehow did not falter" — 但母语者觉得这样冗长。简洁就是力量。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-08">
<h4>C-08 · so + aux 倒装替代</h4>
<p class="coh-en">If the dollar ever falters, <strong>so will</strong> most of the world's price stability.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-095">S-095</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">美元一旦动摇,世界大多数地方的物价稳定,亦将随之动摇。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> "so will" = "will [also falter]"(falter 完全省略,只剩 will)。用于"X 也如此"。否定形式用 "neither/nor + aux + S"。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-09">
<h4>C-09 · 平行省略中的隐含替代</h4>
<p class="coh-en">By 2030, every major central bank will have launched, or at least <strong>tested</strong>, its own central-bank digital currency.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-067">S-067</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">到 2030 年,每一家主要央行,都将已经推出 — 或至少曾试验过 — 自己的央行数字货币。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> "tested" 替代了"will have launched its own CBDC"中除主动词外的全部结构(will have 被省略,只剩 tested)。这种"动词替代 + 平行省略"组合,是英文写作里的高级技巧。</p>
</div>

</div>

<div class="coh-section" id="ellipsis">
<h2>③ 省略衔接 · Ellipsis</h2>
<p class="section-intro">明示性省略 — 关系代词、连词、主语、谓语等被略去,但读者能从上下文百分百补出。</p>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-10">
<h4>C-10 · 关系代词 that 省略(零关系)</h4>
<p class="coh-en">On Saturday mornings, a country shopkeeper would shave a thin sliver off any silver dollar <strong>he did not trust</strong>.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-008">S-008</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">每逢周六上午,乡间店主总会从他所不信任的银元上削下一片薄薄的银屑。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> 完整形式应是 "any silver dollar [<strong>that</strong>] he did not trust",但 that 在宾格关系代词位置可以省略。这是英语口语和书面语都极常见的省略,中国学生写作时往往不敢省。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-11">
<h4>C-11 · Nor 倒装省略前置否定</h4>
<p class="coh-en"><strong>Nor</strong> did the country recover quickly: another decade of stagnation lay ahead.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-027">S-027</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">这个国家也并未迅速复苏 — 又一个十年的停滞,正横亘在前。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> Nor = and + not + (additionally)。一个 nor 字省略了"加一句否定的连接逻辑"。常用于"前一句已讲一个负面结果,现在又追加另一个"的场景。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-12">
<h4>C-12 · 平行结构中的连词省略</h4>
<p class="coh-en">Borrowers wept <strong>and</strong> farmers went bankrupt, yet bond traders cheered.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-049">S-049</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">借款人痛哭,农人破产,而债券交易员却在欢呼。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> 三个分句用 "and ... yet ..." 串联,避免了句号 + 大写新句的中断感。注意母语者很少全用句号 — 中国学生倾向于"一句一句号",看上去像电报稿。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-13">
<h4>C-13 · 时态助动词省略</h4>
<p class="coh-en">If the dollar ever falters, so <strong>will</strong> most of the world's price stability.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-095">S-095</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">美元一旦动摇,世界大多数地方的物价稳定,亦将随之动摇。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> 完整应为 "so will most of the world's price stability [falter]" — falter 完全省略。这是英语"so + aux + S"句型的核心威力:用一个助动词承载完整的语义。</p>
</div>

</div>

<div class="coh-section" id="conjunction">
<h2>④ 连接词衔接 · Conjunction</h2>
<p class="section-intro">显性的逻辑词,告诉读者两句之间的关系 — 是顺承、转折、因果还是时序。这是衔接里最容易学但最容易用错的一类。</p>

<h3>4.1 Additive 顺承(并列、递进)</h3>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-14">
<h4>C-14 · Both ... and ... 双主语并列</h4>
<p class="coh-en"><strong>Both</strong> Britain <strong>and</strong> France borrowed heavily from New York bankers, and the balance of monetary power began to shift across the Atlantic.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-023">S-023</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">英国与法国均向纽约银行家大举借贷,货币力量的天平也由此开始横跨大西洋,向西倾移。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> Both ... and ... 把两个并列主语包成一个集合,谓语用复数(borrowed)。比直接说 "Britain borrowed and France borrowed" 紧凑许多。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-15">
<h4>C-15 · not only ... but also ... 递进</h4>
<p class="coh-en">The Plaza Accord of 1985 was <strong>not only</strong> an attempt to weaken the dollar, <strong>but also</strong> a quiet admission that even superpowers must compromise.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-051">S-051</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">1985 年的《广场协议》,既是一次对美元的有意压低,亦是一种无声的承认 — 即便是超级大国,亦须妥协。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> 标准的"递进"模式 — 后半句的内容比前半句更深、更出乎意料。中国学生喜欢一直用 "and" 连接,缺乏层次。学会用 not only ... but also ... 立刻显得高一档。</p>
</div>

<h3>4.2 Adversative 转折</h3>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-16">
<h4>C-16 · Although 让步从句</h4>
<p class="coh-en"><strong>Although</strong> Bryan lost the election, his oratory still echoes through American memory.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-018">S-018</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">尽管布莱恩输掉了那场选举,他的雄辩之词至今仍在美国的集体记忆里回响。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> Although 引导让步,主句用一般陈述。中国学生常错写成 "Although ..., but ..." — 但英语里 although 和 but **不能同时**出现。要么 although 要么 but,二选一。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-17">
<h4>C-17 · yet 转折(比 but 更书面)</h4>
<p class="coh-en">Borrowers wept and farmers went bankrupt, <strong>yet</strong> bond traders cheered.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-049">S-049</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">借款人痛哭,农人破产,而债券交易员却在欢呼。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> yet ≈ but,但 yet 更书面、更文学化,常见于商业评论和散文。日常对话用 but,Forbes / Economist 长文里 yet 出现频率高。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-18">
<h4>C-18 · paradoxically 副词转折</h4>
<p class="coh-en">The more the world distrusts its own governments, the more it seems, <strong>paradoxically</strong>, to trust the dollar.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-091">S-091</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">这世界对本国政府的不信任愈深,反而对美元的信任愈重 — 不啻一个悖论。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> paradoxically 是"反讽副词",插入主句中部以逗号隔开,标记一种"违反直觉"的转折。比直接说 "but it trusts the dollar" 更精准、更有思考分量。</p>
</div>

<h3>4.3 Causal 因果</h3>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-19">
<h4>C-19 · 不定式表目的</h4>
<p class="coh-en">The greenback was issued in 1862 <strong>to finance</strong> a war the Treasury could no longer pay for in coin.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-013">S-013</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">1862 年发行的"绿背钞",意在为一场国库已无法以硬币支撑的战争筹措军费。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> "to + 动词原形"是英语表目的最简洁的方式。比中文式的 "in order to" / "for the purpose of" 简练得多。中国学生爱用复杂结构,母语者用 to-不定式。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-20">
<h4>C-20 · in order that 正式目的</h4>
<p class="coh-en">Banks were closed for four days, <strong>in order that</strong> public confidence might begin to recover.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-031">S-031</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">银行停业四天 — 以图使公众的信心得以缓步复苏。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> in order that + that 从句(常带情态 might/may/can)是高度正式的目的连接,用于法律、政府、学术语境。日常说 "so that" 即可。</p>
</div>

<h3>4.4 Temporal 时序</h3>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-21">
<h4>C-21 · By + 时间锚点(过去完成的标志)</h4>
<p class="coh-en"><strong>By 1811</strong>, Spanish reales had circulated through the thirteen colonies for nearly a century.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-005">S-005</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">至 1811 年,西班牙银元已在十三块殖民地流通了将近一个世纪。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> "By + 年份"是过去完成时(had + V-en)的天然搭档。这一组合在历史叙事中极频繁。中国学生常误用一般过去时,语义不准。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-22">
<h4>C-22 · When + 时间从句</h4>
<p class="coh-en"><strong>When</strong> the First World War broke out, European gold flowed into American vaults at an unprecedented rate.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-021">S-021</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">第一次世界大战爆发之时,欧洲的黄金以前所未有的速率涌入美国金库。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> "When + 从句, 主句"是叙事最基本的时间衔接。注意从句和主句都可以用一般过去时(同时发生)或完成时(从句先,主句后)。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-23">
<h4>C-23 · Only when + 倒装(只有当...才)</h4>
<p class="coh-en"><strong>Only when</strong> France began demanding bullion for its dollars did the cracks become visible.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-039">S-039</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">直到法国开始为它手中的美元索要金条之时,裂缝方才显现。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> "Only when X, did Y" 是带强调的时间衔接。Only when 前置后,主句必须主谓倒装(did the cracks become)。这是中国学生最难掌握的英语句型之一,但写作里用对一次,立刻提升一档。</p>
</div>

<h3>4.5 Discourse markers 语篇衔接副词</h3>

<div class="coh-case" id="c-24">
<h4>C-24 · Indeed 强化前文</h4>
<p class="coh-en"><strong>Indeed</strong>, Alan Greenspan presided over a period now called the Great Moderation, when markets seemed to demand only the gentlest of nudges.</p>
<p class="coh-source">来源:<a href="/breakdown/#s-056">S-056</a></p>
<p class="coh-zh">诚然,艾伦·格林斯潘所主政的那段时期,如今被称为"大缓和"时代 — 那是一段市场仿佛只需最轻微的拨动便足以平稳运转的岁月。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>用法提示:</b> Indeed 用于"承认前文之上再加一层例证"。比 "And ..." 强烈得多,显得作者有立场。同类:Moreover, Furthermore, In fact, Crucially。</p>
</div>

</div>

<div class="coh-section" id="lexical">
<h2>⑤ 词汇衔接 · Lexical Cohesion</h2>
<p class="section-intro">同义词链、上下位词、反复 — 这是写作里最隐形也最强大的衔接手段。中国学生最大的写作问题:同一个名词反复出现,缺乏词汇变换。母语者会在同义词链中游走,保持指代一致而避免重复。</p>

<h3>5.1 美元同义词链(全文反复使用)</h3>

<div class="coh-chain">
<h4>"美元"在 108 句中的同义词替换链</h4>
<p>同一个对象,母语者会用以下不同表达,根据语境精确选择:</p>
<ol>
<li><code>dollar</code> — 通称(<a href="/breakdown/#s-001">S-001</a>)</li>
<li><code>silver dollar</code> — 早期实体(<a href="/breakdown/#s-002">S-002</a>)</li>
<li><code>the greenback</code> — 内战时期的纸币别名(<a href="/breakdown/#s-013">S-013</a>)</li>
<li><code>every American banknote</code> — 现代钞票(<a href="/breakdown/#s-020">S-020</a>)</li>
<li><code>paper money</code> — 纸币泛称(<a href="/breakdown/#s-012">S-012</a>)</li>
<li><code>a credible reserve currency</code> — 储备货币身份(<a href="/breakdown/#s-022">S-022</a>)</li>
<li><code>fiat currency</code> — 法定货币(<a href="/breakdown/#s-089">S-089</a>)</li>
<li><code>this single piece of paper</code> — 哲学化指代(<a href="/breakdown/#s-077">S-077</a>)</li>
<li><code>the language of global trade</code> — 隐喻指代(<a href="/breakdown/#s-099">S-099</a>)</li>
</ol>
<p style="margin-top:0.7em;color:#555;"><b>启示:</b> 写一篇关于"美元"的文章,如果通篇只用 "the dollar" 一个词,读者会感到单调。母语者会根据**这一句强调什么属性**,选择最合适的同义词。这是英语写作最考验词汇库存的一层。</p>
</div>

<h3>5.2 时间锚点链(历史叙事的脊柱)</h3>

<div class="coh-chain">
<h4>108 句中的时间锚点(从 1792 到 2030+)</h4>
<p>历史散文最重要的衔接手段是时间词。母语者会用<strong>不同形式</strong>的时间锚点,而不是机械重复"In year X":</p>
<p><code>In 1792</code> · <code>By 1811</code> · <code>For two decades</code> · <code>On Saturday mornings</code> · <code>By the time</code> · <code>Within weeks</code> · <code>For two decades</code> · <code>By the late 1960s</code> · <code>On August 15, 1971</code> · <code>Year after year</code> · <code>For a moment</code> · <code>By the mid-1980s</code> · <code>In those years</code> · <code>By 1995</code> · <code>By the time you finish reading this article</code> · <code>Today</code> · <code>By 2030</code> · <code>Within a decade</code></p>
<p style="margin-top:0.7em;color:#555;"><b>启示:</b> 时间锚点的多样化让读者在时间维度上"跳跃"自如。中国学生写时间常只用 "In 2008" / "In 2010",显得机械。变换形式(by / within / for / on / today / now)是衔接的隐形脊柱。</p>
</div>

<h3>5.3 反义对照链 · gold ↔ paper money</h3>

<div class="coh-chain">
<h4>黄金与美元的反复对照</h4>
<p>整篇文章建立在 <code>gold</code>(实物锚) ↔ <code>paper money / dollar</code>(信用锚)的张力上。这一对反义词在多个句子中反复出现,构成全文最深的词汇衔接网:</p>
<ol>
<li><a href="/breakdown/#s-007">S-007</a> 农人携带"tobacco notes, Spanish coin, and even gold dust"</li>
<li><a href="/breakdown/#s-009">S-009</a> 美元含 "371.25 grains of pure silver"</li>
<li><a href="/breakdown/#s-015">S-015</a> "Returning to the gold standard"</li>
<li><a href="/breakdown/#s-032">S-032</a> "would still hang from a chain of gold"</li>
<li><a href="/breakdown/#s-037">S-037</a> "no one ask for gold"</li>
<li><a href="/breakdown/#s-039">S-039</a> "France began demanding bullion for its dollars"</li>
<li><a href="/breakdown/#s-040">S-040</a> "America's postwar gold had been spent abroad"</li>
<li><a href="/breakdown/#s-090">S-090</a> "gold finally untethered"</li>
<li><a href="/breakdown/#s-096">S-096</a> "Not until the gold standard collapsed"</li>
<li><a href="/breakdown/#s-098">S-098</a> "There was no easy way back to gold"</li>
</ol>
<p style="margin-top:0.7em;color:#555;"><b>启示:</b> 一篇好文章的"主题词"应该在不同位置以不同形式反复出现,构成隐形的话题张力。中国学生写作时,常忘记这种"主题词反复"是 cohesion 的核心 — 不是机械重复,而是**有节奏地呼应**。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-section" id="paragraph-demo">
<h2>综合示范 · 一段话里的衔接合奏</h2>
<p class="section-intro">最后,看一段从首页文章中抽出的真实段落,标出所有 cohesion 手段,体会它们如何共同协作。</p>

<div class="coh-case">
<h4>原段(S-039 至 S-042)</h4>
<p class="coh-en"><strong>Only when</strong> France began demanding bullion for <strong>its</strong> dollars did the cracks become visible. <strong>By the late 1960s</strong>, more than half of America's postwar gold had been spent abroad. <strong>On August 15, 1971</strong>, Richard Nixon announced what would later be called the Nixon Shock. The dollar has floated freely <strong>ever since</strong>.</p>
<p class="coh-zh">直到法国开始为它手中的美元索要金条之时,裂缝方才显现。至 1960 年代末,美国战后黄金的过半数,已被花费在海外。1971 年 8 月 15 日,理查德·尼克松宣布的,正是日后被史家称为"尼克松冲击"的那件事。自那时起,美元便一直自由浮动至今。</p>
<p class="coh-tip"><b>这一段动用了 5 种衔接:</b><br>
① <strong>时序连接</strong>(Only when / By the late 1960s / On August 15, 1971 / ever since)— 4 个时间锚点串联起 5 年的危机过程<br>
② <strong>指代</strong>(its dollars 回指 France's,后续 the dollar 持续指代主对象)<br>
③ <strong>词汇衔接</strong>(bullion → gold → the Nixon Shock)— gold 的不同表达<br>
④ <strong>反义对照</strong>(gold standard ↔ floated freely 的隐性对照)<br>
⑤ <strong>段落级倒装强调</strong>(Only when 引发主谓倒装,把读者注意力锁在"裂缝显现"这个关键节点)<br>
<br>
<b>关键洞察:</b> 这 4 句话总长约 60 个英文词,但通过 5 种衔接,**密度极高地讲完了一个完整的危机叙事**。中国学生写同样信息往往要用 100+ 词。**节俭来自衔接,不来自缩短**。</p>
</div>

<div class="coh-tips" id="tips">
<h3>给中国学生的写作建议</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>不要省略所有衔接词。</strong> 中国学生最常见的问题是 "句子之间没逻辑词" — 看上去像电报。每两到三句之间,至少要有一个连接词或时间锚点。</li>
<li><strong>不要过度依赖 however / but / and。</strong> 这三个词不是不能用,但如果一段话里 "and ... but ... however ... and ..." 反复出现,显得贫乏。学会用 yet / paradoxically / indeed / moreover / nevertheless / by then / in those years。</li>
<li><strong>用同义词链,不要重复同一个名词。</strong> "the dollar" 在一段里出现 5 次以上,就要换成 the currency / it / the greenback / America's reserve money 等。</li>
<li><strong>大胆省略关系代词 that。</strong> "the man [that] he met" 是地道写法,中国学生倾向于补全。该省时省。</li>
<li><strong>段落开头用时间锚点。</strong> "In 1933, ..." / "By the late 1960s, ..." / "Today, ..." — 这是最简单也最有效的段落起手式。中国学生常用 "There is..." 开头,显得 weak。</li>
<li><strong>把因果用在该用的地方。</strong> 不要只用 "because" / "so",试试 "as a result" / "in turn" / "consequently" / "by the same token"。</li>
<li><strong>读完一篇好的英语评论,先看它的衔接词。</strong> Forbes / Economist / The Atlantic 的文章,80% 的写作功夫在 cohesion。看一遍这一页的 26 个案例,再读一篇 Economist,你会发现衔接词的存在密度比你想象的大得多。</li>
</ol>
</div>
